Letter · Application

Dear Ellen,

Once again welcome to this application and thank you for taking your time to read it. When I read your listing it was clear to me that you are a person who cares deeply, and I immediately thought "oh, there's a person behind this home, and I'd like to know her." So let me put into words who I am as a person to get you a better understanding about who I would be as a potential housemate ...

I'm creative at heart and a deeply caring person, about the people in my life, about the planet, about doing the small things that make others feel seen. I'm queer, I'm endlessly curious about art, music, ideas, languages and the way other people see the world, and I genuinely love learning new things, talking about them, and helping where I can. I work with my hands a lot (sewing, drawing, clay, small wood projects) and home is where all of that lives. For me, home is also where I recharge, so I really value it being a calm, peaceful place. Somewhere I look forward to coming back to, and somewhere I take care of properly.

I've lived in the Netherlands three times before, including eight months in Rotterdam on exchange at RSM, and over the years I've built a network here of both internationals and Dutch friends who feel like chosen family. Coming to Rotterdam feels like coming home, and I'm planning to stay long-term. This isn't a let's-try-it-out-move for me, it's more of a homecoming.

However, as you can imagine, moving abroad is not just an easy fix and right now I'm in the slightly chaotic in-between of a big move. In the beginning of this year, I finished my master's in human resource management at Copenhagen Business School after a year deepdive in my thesis on inclusion of neurodivergent employees in office work. Ideally, diversity and inclusion is the work I want to spend my life on and what I am very passionate about. My days at the moment are a mix of wrapping up my life in CPH, last coffees with friends, and a sewing project I keep promising myself I'll finish before the move haha!

By the end of July, I hope my mornings start with coffee, settled into a Dutch HR role, hopefully one that actually lets me work on inclusion. I want a life that's quiet, creative, and full of the people I love.

I'm someone who deeply understands that people live and do things differently. I've spent a long time, both academically and personally, thinking about that. All I ask in return is honest, clear communication and genuine respect, and I'll bring the same. I think the small everyday things, like saying when something's bothering you, being considerate around quiet hours, and sharing the cleaning without keeping score, are what make a shared home feel safe rather than tense.

Thank you for writing such a thoughtful ad, by the way. That kind of care is rare, and it really does make a difference. I'd love to come see the place.

– Cami 🌱
